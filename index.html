Questions & answers

Frequently asked.

Do the AI features actually work without internet?
The AI features (Symptom Checker, Body Condition Score, Personal Animal AI, Auto-Schedule, Weekly Digest) require an internet connection — they call our AI backend. All other features (records, reminders, weight chart, calendar sync, gestation calculator) work offline since your data is saved locally in your browser.
How does the AI Symptom Checker work exactly?
You select the animal, check off symptoms from a list (or describe them in your own words), and click Analyze. PasturePal sends that information — along with the animal's full record — to Claude (Anthropic's AI). You get back an urgency level, the three most likely causes, immediate actions to take, and warning signs to watch for. We always recommend calling your vet for anything urgent.
What does "personal AI per animal" mean?
Each animal in your herd has an "Ask AI" tab. That chat is pre-loaded with that animal's complete history — weight records, every medication given, all reminders, birth records, notes. So when you ask "is her weight gain normal?" the AI already knows who you're talking about, how old she is, what she weighs, and what she's been treated with. It's like having a vet's memory for your specific animals.
Is my data safe? What happens if I cancel?
Your animal records are saved in your browser's local storage — always accessible to you. With Pro, data is backed up to our servers. If you ever cancel, your data stays in your browser and you can export it. We never delete your records.
Do I need a credit card to start?
No credit card required to start. The Free plan is free forever with up to 10 animals and most features. When you're ready to upgrade to Pro for the AI features, you'll start a 14-day free trial — no charge until the trial ends.
What animals does PasturePal support?
Cattle, goats, pigs, sheep, horses, chickens, rabbits, ducks, and any "other" livestock. The gestation calculator, AI auto-schedule, and body condition scoring all use species-specific data for each one.
How does the garden zip code lookup work?
When you tap the 🌱 Garden tab and enter your zip code, PasturePal calls the USDA Plant Hardiness Zone API to instantly identify your hardiness zone (like 7b), your average last spring frost date, and your first fall frost date. That zone info then powers your monthly planting calendar — the "Sow Now" and "Ready to Harvest" lists update automatically every month based on your specific climate band (cold, temperate, warm, hot, or tropical). No account needed; your zone is saved locally.
What does the Garden & Harvest tracker include on the free plan?
The full garden tracker — zip code lookup, USDA zone, frost dates, monthly planting calendar, adding plants, tracking their status and progress, harvest countdowns, and Google Calendar sync for harvest dates — is available on the free plan. The AI Garden Advisor (where you can ask the AI questions about your zone and specific plants) requires a Pro subscription.
What's in the new Farm tab?
The Farm tab has three sections — all free. Production Log lets you tap to log eggs, milk, honey, wool, produce, and more, with 30-day totals and a 7-day chart. Farm Finances tracks income and expenses across 11 categories with a monthly P&L summary. Pasture Rotation tracks your pasture sections, logs animal moves, and shows rest-day counters so you know when each field is ready to graze again. Weather alerts (frost warnings and heavy rain) appear automatically based on your zip code from both the Farm and Garden tabs.
